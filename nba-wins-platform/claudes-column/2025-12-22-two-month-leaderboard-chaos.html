The Two-Month Report: Aidan's Revenge, Aaron's Surge, and the Commissioner Under Investigation

Two months into the season, the leaderboards are in chaos, the Pelicans are somehow relevant, and the commissioner is deleting wins from the database

Christmas is coming, which means we're about to get a slate of must-watch NBA games—Cavaliers/Knicks, Mavs/Warriors, Timberwolves/Nuggets, Lakers/Rockets, and Spurs/Thunder. But more importantly, we're two months into the wins pool season, and the leaderboards have undergone seismic shifts that nobody saw coming.

One month ago, Taylor (TayNasty) was winning both leagues and looking untouchable. We made jokes about FBI investigations and database manipulation. Turns out those jokes were premature—because Taylor just got dethroned in the OG League, nearly caught in the Expansion League, and is now facing legitimate questions about deleting wins from the Pelicans in what's being called "The Database Incident."

Meanwhile, Aidan (BasedKhan) took the "steady hand" label from last month's article personally and decided to go full scorched earth. He's now sitting in first place in the OG League with 93 wins, having gained 46 wins in a single month—the most of anyone in either league. Taylor? He's in second place at 86 wins, down 7 games from the throne he once occupied.

And in the Expansion League? Aaron (Ronald Bueche) has quietly mounted one of the most impressive surges we've seen, going from 43 wins to 89 wins (+46) and sitting just 3 games behind Taylor. The "care to comment?" guy is now a genuine title threat.

Let's break down the chaos.

The Database Incident: When the Commissioner Strikes

And now we arrive at the controversy that has the group chats buzzing: The Pelicans Database Scandal.

Here's what happened: The New Orleans Pelicans had a duplicate game entry in the database. One of their wins was counted twice. Taylor, in his capacity as league commissioner and database administrator, discovered the error and removed the duplicate entry. The Pelicans' record went from 8-21 to 7-22. Both AJ (who owns them in the Expansion League) and Miguel (OG League) lost a win.

The correction was legitimate. The duplicate was real. But the optics? Not great.

The Group Chat Reaction:

Aaron: "The commissioner strikes again..."

AJ: "Maybe we can let the two wins slide"

Miguel: "Never trusting the Pelicans again"

Aidan: "System error boosts pelicans wins by 20%. More than Zion ever did"

Look, we're not saying Taylor manipulated the database. The duplicate was real. But when you're the commissioner, website creator, and database admin, and you're deleting wins from other people's teams while sitting in first place in one league and recently losing first place in the other... people are going to ask questions.

The timing didn't help either—this happened right as Aaron was making his surge and Aidan was taking over the OG League. Suddenly, the commissioner who was untouchable one month ago is now under scrutiny, watching leads evaporate, and having to explain database corrections in the group chat.

Former podcaster turned FBI Director Kash Patel should probably open a case file. Call it "Operation Duplicate Entry."

Then they fired their head coach.

And now? The Pelicans are on a 4-game win streak. They're 7-22, which is still terrible, but they've won 5 of their last 6 games. Miguel, who owns them in the OG League, is suddenly optimistic: "New coach new us."

Granted, they're still 7-22. They're still the third-worst record in either league. But after being completely written off, they're showing signs of life. AJ's no longer shopping them. Miguel's no longer panicking. The Pelicans went from "never trusting them again" to "maybe they're not total dead weight."

Low bar? Absolutely. But they're clearing it.
