Here's the thing about this trade deadline that's going to absolutely wreck some win projections: several of the biggest names moved are either hurt, refusing to play, or underwent season-ending surgery immediately after being traded. It's like buying a house and then finding out the foundation is collapsing and also there's a ghost problem and the previous owner salted the earth.

The Wizards acquired AD from Dallas in a massive 9-player, 3-team deal that also involved Charlotte. This should be franchise-altering. AD is a top-10 player when healthy, a defensive monster, a walking 25-and-10.

The problem? He's not playing this season.

Davis has a hand ligament injury that's expected to keep him out for the entire remainder of 2025-26. He played only 20 games for Dallas before the trade. The Wizards essentially acquired a very expensive get-well card that they'll have to stash on the shelf until next October.

Trae Young to Washington: The Other Ghost

In a separate deal back in early January, the Wizards acquired Trae Young from Atlanta for CJ McCollum and Corey Kispert. This was supposed to be the backcourt centerpiece of Washington's rebuild.

But Trae's dealing with an MCL sprain and quad contusion, and the coaching staff says he's "not close to returning." Translation: he'll play "very little, if at all" the rest of the season. He played only 10 games for Atlanta before the trade.

So Washington now has two franchise players—AD and Trae—who won't actually play together until 2026-27. The Wizards aren't tanking. They're not even rebuilding. They're in some kind of basketball purgatory where they acquired a future that doesn't help their present at all.

Jaren Jackson Jr. to Utah: Three Games and Done

Memphis traded JJJ to Utah on February 3rd for three first-round picks in a full-rebuild move. Jackson played exactly three games for the Jazz before undergoing season-ending knee surgery (PVNS removal). He's out for the remainder of 2025-26.

Utah now has zero contribution from their big deadline acquisition and gets to keep their top-8 protected pick since they'll finish with one of the league's worst records.

This is the equivalent of ordering a pizza, taking three bites, and then having your dentist call to tell you that you need emergency root canal surgery right now and can't eat solid foods for six months. Great investment for the future, absolutely useless for today.

D'Angelo Russell to Washington: "I'm Good, Actually"

In the most predictable development of deadline week, D'Angelo Russell—who was sent to Washington as part of a different deal—announced he will not report to the team and is seeking a buyout.

Can't blame him. Washington's roster currently consists of two superstars who won't play, several young guys getting development minutes, and whatever DLo would have been (which is "not interested").

The Wizards are currently 14-39 and pacing for 22 wins. They're not a basketball team right now. They're a tax shelter with a mascot.
