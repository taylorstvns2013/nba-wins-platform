The Pre-Playoff Power Rankings: All 30 NBA Teams Ranked, Roasted, and Ready for April

The NBA Playoffs start Saturday, April 18. Here are all 30 teams sorted into seven tiers of contention, delusion, and existential crisis—plus the wins pool implications that actually matter to the nine people reading this

Every year around this time, Bill Simmons used to sort the entire NBA into tiers—separating the contenders from the pretenders, the pretenders from the play-in hopefuls, and the play-in hopefuls from the teams whose fans have already switched to baseball. It was always my favorite column of the year. The rankings where you stop pretending and just say what you actually think.

So with the NBA Playoffs tipping off on Saturday, April 18—four weeks from today—and most teams sitting on 12-14 games remaining, I'm doing the same thing. All 30 teams. Seven tiers. Reverse order, because anticipation is a beautiful thing and the Wizards deserve to be mentioned first for once in their miserable lives.

But first, a quick check on the two races that actually matter around here—the wins pool standings:

OG League: Taylor (230) has opened up a 16-game lead over Josh (214), with Miguel (207), Aidan (203), and Gage (191) trailing behind. The commissioner's grip on this league has gone from "comfortable" to "oppressive." The rest of the field is playing for second place and everyone knows it.

Expansion League: Taylor (221) leads Aaron (219) by just TWO GAMES. Max (212) is 9 back and surging. AJ (204) and Brian (189) are fading into the sunset. This is a two-horse race with a dark horse closing fast, and it's going down to the final week of the season.

Now let's rank some basketball teams.

TIER 7: "These Guys Are Still Playing?"

30. Washington Wizards (16-53)

There's tanking, and then there's whatever the Wizards have been doing this season—which is less "strategic losing" and more "organizational performance art." Washington is 16-53 and pacing for 19 wins. They acquired Anthony Davis and Trae Young—two franchise-altering talents—and neither one is actually playing basketball for them this season. AD has a hand ligament injury that shut him down. Trae's dealing with an MCL sprain and quad contusion and is "not close to returning." D'Angelo Russell got traded there and immediately requested a buyout because even D'Angelo Russell has standards.

The Wizards aren't tanking. They're not rebuilding. They're existing in a basketball purgatory where they spent real assets acquiring a future that doesn't arrive until October 2026. They're a tax shelter with a mascot.

Back in January, Brian (brianshane.com) asked in the Expansion League chat about how protected draft picks work and whether the NBA was encouraging tanking. His conclusion after the explanation? "Well fuck the Wizards." That quote has aged like a fine wine. Brian owns the Wizards in the Expansion League. Josh (DJ Jazzy Josh) owns them in the OG League. Neither man has recovered from the experience. Josh once declared "Better watch out! Win now Wizards have arrived!" after the AD trade. That's the energy of a man who has accepted his fate and chosen comedy as a coping mechanism.

Wins Pool Impact: The Wizards are contributing 16 wins to both Brian (Expansion) and Josh (OG)—the lowest contribution of any team to any owner across both leagues. When your Round 6 pick is pacing for 19 wins, you didn't draft a team. You drafted a charitable donation.

29. Sacramento Kings (18-53)

The Kings are 18-53 and pacing for 21 wins, which is somehow only the third-worst record in the league. Vegas had them at 35.5 wins. They're tracking at 20.8. That's a -14.7 miss against the over/under, the second-worst variance in basketball behind the Pacers.

Remember in the draft alignment article way back in October when we talked about which teams "absolutely no one wants to touch"? The Kings weren't one of them. They were supposed to be a mid-round pick with playoff upside. Instead, they've become the embodiment of the phrase "it can always get worse."

Wins Pool Impact: Miguel (Sweet Dick Miggy) owns them in the OG League. Max (Max Bent) owns them in the Expansion League. Both men drafted the Kings in Round 5 hoping for 35 wins and got 21. That's not a draft bust. That's a draft felony. Miguel's top-3 of Pistons/Spurs/Rockets is carrying him through the wreckage, and Max's Spurs/Celtics/Rockets are doing the same, but the Kings are an anchor that neither man needed.

TIER 3.5: "Buzz City Madness"

14. Charlotte Hornets (36-34)

The Hornets get their own tier because what they've done since January 1 defies categorization.

Let me set the scene: Charlotte started the season 4-14. They fell to 13-25. They were supposed to be tanking for AJ Dybantsa, Darryn Peterson, or Cam Boozer. Vegas had them at 27.5 wins. The over/under was basically "pick your favorite ping-pong ball." And then something happened.

Since January 1, the Hornets are tied with Boston for the most wins in the league. They have the NBA's best net rating at +12.0. The best offensive rating at 122.0. The fifth-best defensive rating. The best team three-point percentage at 39.3%. Their starting five of LaMelo Ball, Brandon Miller, Kon Knueppel, Miles Bridges, and Moussa Diabate are 20-2 when they play together with a +27.9 net rating. Knueppel—a rookie—has broken the rookie three-point record and is a legitimate Rookie of the Year candidate.

An SI piece compared them to the 2022 Celtics, who went from 18-21 to making the NBA Finals after a similar mid-season switch flipped. The parallels are eerie: both teams went on a nine-game win streak from late January to early February, and both eventually lost that streak... to the Detroit Pistons.

Charlotte is +14.7 against their Vegas over/under—the highest positive variance of any team in the NBA. They went from lottery-bound to above .500, from tanking to terrifying, from "these guys are still playing?" to "wait, should we be worried about them?"

In the OG League chat on March 4, after the Hornets beat the Celtics 118-89—a 29-point demolition of the 4th-best team in the league—Aidan demanded answers: "Claude need to look into how Taylor's FINAL pick beat the 4th best team in the league by 30 POINTS."

Taylor's response was a FanDuel screenshot showing he'd placed a $1 bet on the Hornets beating the Celtics and won. His caption: "Let's just say I seen it coming."

The man owns the database, built the website, drafted the Hornets with his last pick in both leagues, and bet real money on them beating the Celtics by 29. At some point, the conspiracy theories from Article #1 stop being jokes and start being a documentary.

Wins Pool Impact: Taylor owns them in the OG League (pick #27, Round 6). Aaron owns them in the Expansion League (pick #27, Round 6). Both men are getting +14.7 wins of value from their final draft pick. That's the single best value pick relative to draft position in either league. Taylor's Round 6 steal is contributing 36 wins to a roster that leads the OG League by 16 games. Aaron's getting the same 36 wins in a league where he trails Taylor by just 2. The Hornets might literally decide the Expansion League championship.

TIER 1: "Book Your Hotel Rooms Now"

3. Detroit Pistons (50-19)

The Pistons are 50-19, pacing for 59 wins, and +13.9 against their Vegas over/under of 45.5. This is the team that was 15-2 before Thanksgiving, 24-6 by Christmas, and has held the best record in the Eastern Conference for most of the season. Cade Cunningham was averaging 24.5 points, 9.9 assists, and 5.6 rebounds—an MVP-caliber season—before suffering a collapsed lung on Tuesday night against the Wizards.

The Cunningham injury is the biggest story in the Eastern Conference right now. He collided with Tre Johnson while diving for a loose ball, played about five minutes before leaving, and was diagnosed with a left lung pneumothorax.
